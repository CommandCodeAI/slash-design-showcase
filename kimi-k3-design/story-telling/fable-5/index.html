Chapter II · The long way back

Gather gently.

Our botanists harvest by hand, by moonlight, when essential oils crest in the leaf. Never more than one stem in ten. The forest doesn't notice we were there.

Distill cold.

No heat, no solvents, no hurry. Lunar-cycle maceration draws out what fire would destroy — powered entirely by the night wind and the sun it borrows from.

Return everything.

Every vessel comes home to be refilled. Every hectare we touch, we replant threefold. The ledger with the forest always closes in its favour.
