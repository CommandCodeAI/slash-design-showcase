Chapter I The Problem

We built a world of beautiful things —
and billed the cost to the living earth.

Every year, the luxury industry burns through ancient forests, poisons watersheds with dye runoff, and buries a mountain of discarded splendor. Beauty became extraction wearing a silk glove.

92 million tonnes

of textile waste buried or burned each year

20% of clean water

polluted globally by fabric dyeing and finishing

Chapter II The Journey

So we walked back into the forest, and asked what luxury would look like if nature were the atelier.

Listen.

Three years spent with foresters, dyers, and mycologists — learning what the land could offer without being diminished.

Restore.

Every supply chain redesigned as a loop. What we harvest, we replant tenfold. What we make, we guarantee for life — then take back to remake.

Craft.

Objects made slowly, by hands that are paid fairly, from materials that were once alive — and will be allowed to live again.

Chapter III The Solution

An ecosystem of objects, grown rather than extracted.

01 / Material

Regenerative matter

Mycelium leather, algae-dyed silk, and timber harvested only where forests are expanding. Each material is chosen for what it gives back while it grows.

−84% water vs. conventional
02 / Loop

Circular craft

Every LUNAR piece carries a lifetime promise. Return it in a decade and we restore it, remake it, or compost it back into the systems it came from.

0 pieces to landfill, ever
03 / Wild

Renewal fund

A fixed share of every purchase flows directly to rewilding trusts — old-growth corridors, river restoration, and the communities who steward them.

11% of revenue, in perpetuity
