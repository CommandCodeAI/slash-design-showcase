02 — About

I photograph the pause
before the word

Elena Voss began with her grandfather's Leica and a one-way ticket to Lisbon. Sixteen years later, her work lives in the space between documentation and poetry — portraits that breathe, editorials that whisper, landscapes that hold their silence.

Her photographs have appeared in independent print across Europe and in private collections from Copenhagen to Kyoto. She works slowly, shoots sparingly, and believes the best frame is usually the one after everyone relaxes.

“Light doesn't ask permission. Neither does honesty. My job is simply to be there when both arrive.”
