01 — About

The space between pixels and physics.

I'm Alex Rivera. For the past nine years I've been building impossible objects and teaching them to move — fluid sims that behave like fabric, chrome that remembers light, geometry that breathes.

My work lives where art direction meets real-time rendering: launch films, interactive installations, and motion systems for studios like Onyx, Field and Nocturne.

04 — Creative Process

How worlds get built

Phase 01

Listen & Decode

Every world starts with a feeling. We dig into the brand, the audience and the single emotion the film must leave behind.

Phase 02

Sketch in Space

Styleframes, animatics and rough geometry. Composition is decided in 3D from day one — never retrofitted in post.

Phase 03

Motion Sculpting

Simulations, camera choreography, light. This is where the piece learns to breathe — every easing curve tuned by hand.

Phase 04

Ship the World

Final grade, master renders and delivery in every ratio. The world leaves the studio — and starts living on its own.
