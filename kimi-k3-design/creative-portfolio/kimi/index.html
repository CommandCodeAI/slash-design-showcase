02 — About

MV

I build interfaces the way editors build pages — every element earning its place.

For the past seven years I have worked between design studios and engineering teams, which means I speak both dialects fluently. I sketch in Figma and finish in the browser, because the medium has opinions that mockups cannot.

My work has been recognised by Awwwards, FWA and CSS Design Awards. I care about the last 4 percent — the easing curve, the focus ring, the 60fps — because that is where trust is built.

03 — Process

Four moves, no theatre

01

Discover

Every project starts with immersion — your users, your constraints, your ambition. I audit what exists, gather references far outside the industry, and define the single idea the experience must communicate.

  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Content & technical audit
  • Creative brief
02

Design

Art direction first: typography, color, rhythm. Then motion studies — because how a thing moves is part of what it means. I prototype in the browser early so decisions are tested against reality, not against a picture of reality.

  • Art direction
  • Motion studies
  • Browser prototypes
03

Develop

Semantic, accessible, fast. Animation runs on the compositor, layouts hold under the longest German compound noun, and the whole thing ships at a Lighthouse score your competitors will screenshot.

  • Semantic HTML
  • 60fps motion
  • Performance budget
04

Deliver

Launch is a beginning, not an ending. I test across real devices, hand over documentation people actually read, and stay through the first iteration cycle to watch real users meet the work.

  • Device QA
  • Documentation
  • Post-launch iteration
